F R A N K L I N S C H O L A R

The Franklin Scholars are a collective of extraordinary young people, committed to making a difference in their own schools.

As peer mentors, tutors and champions, the Franklin Scholars support younger students through key transitions, powering them up with the skills, habits and mindsets that underpin academic and future success.

WHAT WE DO

WE MOBILISE

They have the knowledge, the experience, and the potential to positively influence others.

We ignite this potential, seeking out our schools' most promising and compassionate Year 9/10 students through an accessible, reflective and rigorous recruitment process.

WE COACH

Our training equips these promising students - the Franklin Scholars - with the skills and tools they need to be emotionally intelligent mentors and focused literacy tutors for Year 7 pupils, as well as effective leaders of their own learning as they gear up for GCSEs.

Everything we do is grounded in evidence of the impact of peer tutoring, to maximise learning outcomes for both groups of students involved in the programme.

WE EQUIP

We provide students and staff with a flexible curriculum framework and a range of tools and on-going support, setting the Franklin Scholars up to take ownership of and deliver effective tutoring and mentoring sessions for Year 7 pupils, and coaching the in-school Programme Leader to develop through running the programme.

All activities, tools and resources are designed to develop literacy skills, but also the growth mindsets, learning strategies and social and emotional literacy that are so crucial to students' success.

WE CONNECT

Beyond our initial training, we deliver a series of additional workshops with the Franklin Scholars over the course of the year, to review pupils' progress and provide further leadership coaching.

And beyond this, we commit to connecting the Franklin Scholars to a range of prestigious and purposeful opportunities with our partner organisations and endorsers. These have most recently included a debate competition at the US Embassy and an exclusive evening with Cambridge University.
